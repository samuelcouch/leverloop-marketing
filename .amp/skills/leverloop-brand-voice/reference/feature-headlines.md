# Feature Headlines & UI Copy Patterns

Approved headline patterns and copy conventions for LeverLoop features.

## Feature Headline Formula

**Lead with the outcome, not the feature name.** The headline tells the reader what changes for them. The subhead can explain the mechanism.

## Approved Feature Headlines

| Feature | Headline |
|---------|----------|
| Speed-to-Lead | New lead? They'll hear from you in 60 seconds. |
| Revenue Recovery | See every dollar you're leaving on the table. |
| Estimate Builder | Three options. One tap. The customer decides from their phone. |
| Dispatch Board | Your whole team. One board. Drag, drop, done. |
| Mobile Tech App | Everything your tech needs — even without a signal. |
| Invoicing & Text-to-Pay | Invoice to cash in minutes, not days. |
| Flat-Rate Pricebook | Every tech quotes like your best tech. |
| "On My Way" Notifications | No more "where's my guy?" calls. Ever. |
| Service Agreements | Turn one-time jobs into monthly revenue. |
| Google Review Requests | Happy customer? Get the review while they're still smiling. |

## Headline Writing Rules

1. **Use concrete numbers** when possible ("60 seconds", "one tap", "minutes not days")
2. **Use second person** — "your tech", "your customer", "you'll see"
3. **Keep it short** — headlines should be one punchy sentence, rarely two
4. **End with impact** — the last word or phrase should land hard
5. **Use em dashes** (—) for dramatic pause, not semicolons or parentheses
6. **Periods are fine** at the end of short declarative headlines ("Drag, drop, done.")

## Subheadline Pattern

Subheadlines expand on the headline with specifics. They should:
- Add one concrete detail or proof point
- Stay under two sentences
- Continue the conversational tone

**Example:**
> **Headline:** Every tech quotes like your best tech.
> **Subheadline:** Your flat-rate pricebook means consistent pricing, professional estimates, and no more guessing in the field. Set it once — every tech uses it from day one.

## CTA Button Copy

CTAs should feel like a next step, not a commitment:

| ✓ Use | ✗ Avoid |
|-------|---------|
| See how much revenue you're missing → | Request a Demo |
| Start your free trial | Get Started Today! |
| Book a 15-minute walkthrough | Schedule a Consultation |
| See it in action | Learn More |
| Try it free — no credit card | Sign Up Now |

## Good/Better/Best Estimate Copy Pattern

When describing the tiered estimate feature, always frame it from the customer's perspective:

> Your tech presents three options — good, better, best. Your customer picks the one that fits. No pressure, no awkward upsell. Just clear choices and a tap to approve.

Never describe it as "upselling" or "maximizing ticket value" in customer-facing copy.
