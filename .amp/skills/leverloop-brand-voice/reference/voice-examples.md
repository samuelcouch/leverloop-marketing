## Product UI Copy

In-product copy: clear, concise, encouraging. Every screen should feel like progress.

### Empty States

Empty states are onboarding moments. Make them feel like the starting line, not a dead end.

| Screen | Copy |
|--------|------|
| Lead Pipeline | No leads yet. Once they start coming in, every single one shows up here — nothing falls through the cracks. |
| Revenue Recovery | Nothing to recover yet. (That's a good sign.) As you send estimates and close jobs, we'll flag every dollar that's slipping through. |
| Dispatch Board | Your board is empty. Add your first job and start building tomorrow's schedule. |
| Service Agreements | No memberships yet. When you're ready, this is where one-time customers become monthly revenue. |
| Customer Database | Your customer list starts here. Every customer, every property, every service record — searchable from anywhere. |
| Equipment Tracking | No equipment logged yet. When your techs start logging units, you'll build the data that makes you irreplaceable. |

### Success & Confirmation Messages

| Action | Copy |
|--------|------|
| Estimate sent | Estimate sent. Your customer can approve it from their phone right now. |
| Invoice paid | Paid in full. That was fast. |
| Lead auto-texted | Lead contacted in 47 seconds. The follow-up sequence is running. |
| Job completed | Job closed out. Great work. |
| Agreement enrolled | New member enrolled. Autopay is set. Recurring revenue: activated. |
| Tech invited | Invite sent. They'll create their account and land right in your team. |

### Error & Edge Cases

| Situation | Copy |
|-----------|------|
| Payment failed | That payment didn't go through. Double-check the card and try again — the invoice is saved. |
| Sync error | QuickBooks sync hit a snag. We'll retry automatically, but you can also re-sync manually here. |
| Offline mode active | You're offline. Everything still works — we'll sync up when you're back in range. |
| Form validation | Missing a phone number. We need it to send the estimate. |

## Outbound Sales Email

Knowledgeable, peer-level, specific. Never generic. Never spammy.

> **Subject:** Quick question about your declined repairs
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> Saw you're running a [X]-truck HVAC shop in [City]. Nice operation.
>
> Quick question — do you know how much revenue is sitting in repairs your customers declined in the last 6 months? For most shops your size, it's somewhere between $8K and $20K.
>
> LeverLoop tracks every declined repair and sends automated follow-ups with financing options, seasonal urgency, and expiring-quote reminders. Most of our users recover 15-25% of that bucket in the first quarter.
>
> Worth a 15-minute look?
>
> [Name]
> LeverLoop
