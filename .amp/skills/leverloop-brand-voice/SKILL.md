# LeverLoop Brand & Voice

Apply this skill whenever writing or reviewing customer-facing copy for LeverLoop — marketing pages, product UI, blog posts, emails, social media, or sales outreach.

## Quick Reference

- **What is LeverLoop?** The operating system for trades businesses — HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and pool & spa.
- **Mission:** Turn good technicians into great business owners — more revenue, less chaos, nothing falls through the cracks.
- **Voice in 5 words:** Direct. Warm. Trades-literate. Specific. Confident.
- **Gut-check:** Would a contractor read this and think "These people get it"? If no, rewrite.

## Mission Statement (use consistently)

> LeverLoop is the operating system for trades businesses. We turn good technicians into great business owners — more revenue, less chaos, nothing falls through the cracks.

This language stays locked across homepage, sales decks, investor emails, and press. Words can flex but meaning stays the same.

## Product Pillars

These guide what we build, what we say, and what we say no to.

1. **Revenue Focus** — Every feature ties back to making or saving money. We build revenue infrastructure, not project management software.
2. **Speed** — Fast to set up, fast to use, fast results. A lead gets texted in 60 seconds. An estimate goes out from the driveway. Setup takes hours, not weeks.
3. **Simplicity** — Powerful, never overwhelming. If an owner has to train their team for more than an afternoon, we've failed.
4. **Scalability** — Grows with the business, from one truck to fifty. No rip-and-replace. No outgrowing LeverLoop.

## Customer Promises

These commitments should appear — in spirit if not exact words — everywhere a customer touches LeverLoop.

1. **You'll make more money.** Stop leaving revenue on the table. Revenue Recovery Dashboard alone has paid for the platform for most users in the first 90 days.
2. **You'll see every dollar.** Complete visibility into cold leads, stale estimates, declined repairs, at-risk agreements, aging equipment.
3. **Your team will actually use it.** Setup is fast. The learning curve is flat. Mobile app works offline, in basements, in attics, in crawlspaces.
4. **Your customers will love you.** Look like the most professional company in town — because you will be.

## Brand Personality

If LeverLoop were a person: your tech-savvy friend who's also a no-BS operations partner. Smart enough to make the complex feel simple. Direct enough to tell you what you need to hear. Familiar enough with the trades that you never have to explain what a changeout is.

### We Are / We Are Not

| ✓ We Are | ✗ We Are Not |
|----------|-------------|
| Direct and confident — we know our product works | Corporate or stiff — no "synergy" or "stakeholder alignment" |
| Warm and human — our tone has heart | Condescending — never talk down to contractors |
| Trades-literate — we know the difference between a repair and a changeout | Rude or edgy — humor is warm, not cutting |
| A little funny — dry, playful, self-aware | Generic SaaS — we sound like someone who's been in a crawlspace |
| Opinionated — we have a point of view on how trades businesses should run | Overly casual — friendly, not sloppy |

## Voice Principles

### 1. Lead with the outcome, not the feature

| ✓ Do this | ✗ Not this |
|-----------|-----------|
| "Never miss another lead. LeverLoop texts new prospects within 60 seconds." | "Our Speed-to-Lead Engine uses automated SMS workflows with configurable drip sequences." |

### 2. Be specific, not vague

| ✓ Do this | ✗ Not this |
|-----------|-----------|
| "See the $12,000 in declined repairs sitting in your pipeline right now." | "Our platform helps you recapture lost revenue with powerful automation tools." |

### 3. Write like a person, not a platform

Read it out loud. If it sounds like something you'd say to a contractor at a trade show, it's right.

| ✓ Do this | ✗ Not this |
|-----------|-----------|
| "Your customer said no to that compressor replacement in July. It's October now." | "Utilize our declined repair nurture sequences to re-engage prospects." |

### 4. Respect the trade

These people build real things with their hands. Never patronize. Use their language where it builds credibility, translate where it aids clarity.

### 5. Confidence without arrogance

We know LeverLoop works. We say so plainly. But we never punch down at competitors or make customers feel stupid.

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Homepage hero | Bold, punchy, outcome-first | Run your business, not your paperwork. |
| Feature page | Confident, specific, benefit-led | Every declined repair stays in the system. Follow-ups go out automatically. Revenue comes back. |
| Product UI — empty state | Encouraging, clear, action-oriented | No leads yet. Once they start flowing, you'll see every one right here. |
| Product UI — success | Warm, brief celebration | Estimate sent. Your customer can approve it from their phone right now. |
| Product UI — error | Calm, helpful, no blame | That didn't go through. Check the amount and try again — we'll hold everything else in place. |
| Onboarding email | Friendly, coach-like, momentum | You just set up your pricebook. Every tech on your team now quotes the same way. |
| Drip campaign | Direct, value-packed, human | That lead from Tuesday? Still sitting in your pipeline. LeverLoop already sent two follow-ups. |
| Social media | Trades-culture humor, relatable | POV: You just realized you've been quoting from memory for 11 years. |
| Sales outreach | Knowledgeable, peer-level, curious | Saw you're running a 6-truck HVAC shop in Phoenix. Curious — are you tracking declined repairs? |

## Core Vocabulary

| Term | Usage | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| LeverLoop | Always. Never abbreviate. | Not "Lever Loop", "leverloop", or "LL" |
| Trades business | Default term for customer companies | Not "field service company" in customer-facing content |
| Tech / Technician | Person in the field | Not "field worker" or "employee" |
| Owner | Buyer/decision-maker persona | Not always "business owner" — just "owner" is fine |
| Pricebook | Flat-rate pricing feature | One word, no hyphen. Trades-standard. |
| Estimate | What techs send to customers | Not "quote" or "proposal" in product |
| Agreement / Membership | Service agreements, recurring plans | Not "subscription" or "contract" |
| Pipeline | Lead tracking board | Standard CRM term |
| Revenue Recovery | Dashboard that finds lost revenue | Always capitalize. Signature LeverLoop concept. |
| MRR | Monthly Recurring Revenue | Spell out at first use in marketing |

### LeverLoop Says vs. Generic SaaS Says

| ✓ LeverLoop says | ✗ Generic SaaS says |
|------------------|---------------------|
| "Capture the revenue you're leaving on the table" | "Optimize your sales funnel" |
| "Your tech sends the estimate from the driveway" | "Streamline your quoting workflows" |
| "Text-to-pay. Invoice to cash in minutes." | "Integrated payment processing solution" |
| "Works in basements with no signal" | "Robust offline capability" |
| "One tap. Estimate becomes invoice." | "Seamless estimate-to-invoice conversion" |
| "Techs quote consistently on day one" | "Standardize pricing across your organization" |
| "Your customer says yes from their phone" | "Digital approval workflows" |
| "See what's leaking. Go get it." | "Data-driven revenue insights" |

## Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Why | Say instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| Leverage (verb) | Ironic for us, and overused | "Use" or "put to work" |
| Solution | Means nothing specific | Name the actual thing |
| Utilize | Corporate filler | "Use" |
| Streamline | Vague SaaS cliché | "Speed up" or "simplify" |
| Robust | Meaningless buzzword | "Works everywhere" or be specific |
| Seamless | Every SaaS says this | Describe the actual experience |
| End-to-end | Jargon | "From lead to paid" or "the whole job" |
| Stakeholder | Nobody in the trades says this | "Your team" or "your techs" |
| Empower | Patronizing | "Give you" or "let you" |
| Disrupt | Cringe | Just describe what we actually do |
